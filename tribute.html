Dr. Alistair Cockburn

The founder of the agile movement and a famous American computer scientist.

A short timeline of Dr.Alistair Cockburn's life:

  • From, 1994, he formed "Humans and Technology" in Salt Lake City.
  • He received his PhD degree from the University of Oslo.
  • Cockburn helped write the Manifesto for Agile Software Development in 2001, the agile PM Declaration of Interdependence in 2005, and co-founded the International Consortium for Agile in 2009.
  • He is a Principal expositoe=r of the use case for documenting business processes and behavioral requirements for software, and inventor of the Cockburn Scale for categorizing software projects.
  • He presented his Hexagonal Architecture in 2005.
  • In 2015, he launched the Heart of Agile movement which is presented as a response to the overly complex state of the Agile Industry.
